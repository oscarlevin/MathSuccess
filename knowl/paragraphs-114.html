The Basics.

Whenever you want to write math, or use symbols, you need to start and end with a dollar sign. So if you type:
Consider the equation \(\$3+2x=7\$\)
A single dollar sign on either end will keep your math in line with the text [shown below]:
Consider the equation \(3+2x=7\)
Display math mode will center your lines of math on their own line. To accomplish this, use 2 dollar signs on either end. So your code will look like:
Consider the equation \(\$\$3+2x=7\$\$\)
The output in latex will look like:
Consider the equation
\begin{align*} 3+2x=7 \amp \end{align*}
Aligned equations will allow you to have multiple lines of equations where your equal signs are aligned:
Resulting output:
\begin{align*} 3+2x \amp =7 \\ 2x \amp =7-3 \end{align*}
Note: the & symbol tells latex to align the next symbol with the previous & symbols. The two backslashes at the end of a line tell latex to end that line and start a new one.
